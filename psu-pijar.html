About the Journal

The Pacific International Journal of Applied Research (PIJAR) is a multidisciplinary, peer-reviewed, open-access journal pioneered by the faculty at Pacific States University. PSU is an accredited institution dedicated to educating, inspiring, and empowering students from around the world through innovative, high-quality on-campus and distance learning programs.

The fundamental objective of PIJAR is to create a scholarly platform for the publication of quality research that contributes meaningfully to various professional and academic fields. The journal seeks to offer students, faculty, and professionals the opportunity to collaborate, share, and advance knowledge across disciplines — including STEM, education, business, health sciences, and technology.

PIJAR embraces the diversity of Pacific States University's global community. Submissions are welcomed from PSU faculty, graduate students, and external scholars whose work aligns with the journal's mission of applied, rigorous, and impactful research.

Scope & Focus Areas

PIJAR welcomes original research articles, review papers, and case studies addressing real-world problems. Current thematic areas of interest include:

  • Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning & Data Science
  • Cybersecurity, Federated Learning & Blockchain Systems
  • Education Technology & Instructional Design
  • UI/UX Research & Human-Computer Interaction
  • Business Analytics & Management Innovation
  • Healthcare Informatics & Applied Life Sciences
  • Social Sciences & Community Development Research

Submit Your Research to PIJAR

We invite original manuscripts from PSU faculty, graduate researchers, and collaborative authors. All submissions are evaluated on scholarly merit, originality, and contribution to the field.

Editorial Board

PIJAR is managed by an editorial board composed of PSU faculty with deep expertise across disciplines. The board upholds the highest standards of academic integrity and supports authors throughout the publication process.

For editorial inquiries, please contact pijar@psu.edu.
